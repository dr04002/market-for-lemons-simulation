Welcome to the Market for Lemons Simulation

This simulation brings to life the market dynamics described in George Akerlof's seminal paper, "The Market for 'Lemons': Quality Uncertainty and the Market Mechanism." In this market, two types of goods are traded: high-quality "Peaches" and low-quality "Lemons."

Buyers and sellers have different information about the quality of these goods, leading to a phenomenon known as "adverse selection," where lower-quality goods tend to dominate the market. As a result, higher-quality goods ("Peaches") are driven out, and the overall quality declines.

Explore the Simulation: Adjust parameters such as market size, prices, quality, and information asymmetry to see how they affect the market outcomes. Observe the equilibrium price, total goods sold, and the proportion of lemons to peaches.

  • Market Size: Number of buyers and sellers in the market.
  • Price of Lemons & Peaches: Prices of low and high-quality goods.
  • Quality of Lemons & Peaches: Utility (value) buyers perceive from each good.
  • Information Mode: Symmetric (everyone knows the quality) or Asymmetric (only sellers know the quality).

Start the simulation and observe how varying levels of information and quality uncertainty affect the market equilibrium!
